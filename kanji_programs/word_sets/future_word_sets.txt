    -> れい is the standard pronunciation for counting, stating there is zero of something, etc
    -> ぜろ is an alternative pronunciation that is more beginner friendly and becoming increasingly popular
    -> まる is used to spell out a list of digits, such as a phone number, address, etc

AGE:
歳/才 - counter for "___ years old", さい
    -> 歳 is the proper kanji for age, used in legal documents
    -> 才 is used for visual recognition, although its actual meaning is something completely different
- Notable exceptions:
- 一歳 - 1 year old, いっさい
- 八歳 - 8 years old, はっさい
- 十歳 - 10 years old, じゅうさい
- *二十歳 - 20 years old, はたち
    -> In Japan, become 20 years old has extra significance - 21-29 is not read as はたち__さい , but にじゅう__さい
